Tamagotchi

The Digital Pet of the 90s.

  • The Tamagotchi is a handheld digital pet game from Japan.
  • It was invented by Aki Maita and created by Akihiro Yokoi in 1996.
  • The name is a portmanteau combining the two Japanese words tamago (たまご), which means "egg", and uotchi (ウオッチ) "watch".
  • It was launched in Japan in 1996, followed by the rest of the world. By 2017, over 82 million units have been sold.
  • The game requires the user to monitor the life cycle of a pet via three button controls and monochrome screen (in later years colour screen versions were available).
  • The pet requires regular cleaning, feeding and playing with the user to ensure a healthy lifecycle.
  • The lifecycle typically ranges from baby to adult, with later versions adding a senior lifestage.
  • With increased popularity of the game, later versions allowed infrared connectivity with other games, to play, form relationships and even marry with other pets.
  • As of 2009, there are over 44 different version of Tamagotchi.
  • Tamagotchi has also been made into films, video games and anime tv shows. Revial of the game in the late 2010's featured a phone app version.
  • At the height of it's popularity, there was controversy over the disruption made to children's education due to the constant care required to avoid the death of the pet. Subsquently, the toy was banned at many schools.
